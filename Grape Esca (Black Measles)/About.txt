Grapevine measles, also called esca, black measles or Spanish measles, has long plagued grape growers with its cryptic expression of symptoms and, for a long time, a lack of identifiable causal organism(s). The name 'measles' refers to the superficial spots found on the fruit 

Symptoms:
Leaf symptoms can be found on canes with infected fruit; however, it is not uncommon for only the leaves or only fruit to show symptoms. Moreover, symptoms are not necessarily expressed every year. The most severe cases seem to occur in years with heavy spring rains, followed by consistently high summertime temperatures.
